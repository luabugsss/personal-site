## Background

This project does not begin as a conventional portfolio. It starts with a narrower problem: many personal sites either feel like job templates or project walls.

## Problem

If the homepage carries too much information, it feels self-promotional. If it becomes too soft, it loses credibility. The balance has to sit between warmth and professional clarity.

## Approach

The site uses a structured personal homepage. The left side creates recognition with an illustration and quick facts; the right side explains current focus and what the site contains. Projects and writing support the story instead of dominating it.

## Outcome

The result is a content-driven site foundation that works as a personal calling card now and can grow with projects and writing later without breaking the narrative.
